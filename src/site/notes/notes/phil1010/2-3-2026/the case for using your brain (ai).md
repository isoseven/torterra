### autonomy vs ai
- technology helps us with automation and mundane tasks
	- concern: intellectual autonomy (thinking **with** us) vs letting devices think **for** us
		- **autonomy** - ability to think for yourself. independence.
- ai therefore hurts our sense of autonomy and our critical thinking.
### background
- humans are **tool-using creatures** with a long history of offloading mental labor
	- phones, calendars, typewriters, printers... etc.
	- even books and written language!
- tools were used in **all** aspects of life
### the extended mind
- the mind extends **beyond** our physical bodies.
- the biological brain couples with the **technology, spaces, and people it interacts with**
	- these notes are parts of my extended mind
	- we outsource our intelligence into other things
- but when should we offload/outsource?
	- intention offloading (+)
	- automating recurring, mundane tasks (+)
