## rhetoric
- **rhetoric** is art of persuasive speaking or writing
	- **four elements** of rhetoric:
		- **ethos**: appeal to credibility
		- **logos**: appeal to rationality
		- **pathos**: appeal to emotion
		- **kairos**: season/opportune time for an argument
	- **five canons** of rhetoric
		- invention: devising the arguments that will make the case convincing.
		- arrangement: ordering argument into logical format
		- style: finding suitable words and figures of speech
		- memory: retaining the argument in mind, including content and arrangement.
		- delivery: effective use of voice and gestures to present argument.
- what is **not rhetoric**?
	- rhetoric has to be an argument made to change someone's mind (as opposed to *using* or coercion.)
	- not used, but **invented**.

> "The first kind depends on the personal character of the speaker; the second on putting the audience in a certain frame of mind; the third on the proof, or apparent proof, provided by the words of the speech itself." - *Rhetoric*, 105-10

## inductive vs deductive
- induction: from example to general rule.
	- examples, *inducting* new examples to rule. evidence based reasoning
		- "all swans i've seen are white, therefore all swans are white"
- deduction: uses logic to get to conclusion
	- uses syllogisms (rhetorical syllogism)
		- major premise: all men are mortal
		- minor premise: socrates is a man
		- conclusion: therefore socrates is mortal
	- relies on logic; if there's a logical fallacy, it falls short.
