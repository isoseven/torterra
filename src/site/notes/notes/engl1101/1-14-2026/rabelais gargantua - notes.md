# 🥫SUMMARY
- Rabelais - Gargantua
	- Gives a snapshot of medieval culture, along with the literacies learned
	- These literacies (knowledge) are also used even today.

# 278-279
--- 
Once Ponocrates had taken cognizance of Gargantua’s vicious manner  
of living, he determined to introduce him to letters by a different  
method, but he let things go on for the first few days considering that  
Nature will not endure sudden change without a strong reaction.
...
While he was being rubbed down, a passage of Holy Scripture was  
read out to him, loud and clear, with a delivery appropriate to the  
matter. A young page was appointed to do it: a native of Basche  
called Anagnosties. Following the theme and content of the lesson,  
Gargantua would often devote himself to revering, worshipping,  
supplicating and adoring God in his goodness, whose majesty and  
marvellous judgments were revealed by the reading.

---
- **Gargantua**: a large guy (lol)
- here we see religious literacy in the second paragraph.
	- about learning the specifics of religion, specifically christianity
# 280
---
At the beginning of the meal a pleasant tale of ancient deeds of  
chivalry would be read until he had taken his wine. Then (if thought  
fit) the reading would be resumed or else they would enter upon  
enjoyable discussions, talking (during the first few months) of the  
qualities, properties, efficacy and nature of all the things that had  
been served up at table: bread, wine, water, salt, flesh, fish, fruit,  
herbs and roots, as well as how to prepare them. In doing so they  
quickly learnt all the relevant passages in Pliny, Athenaeus,  
Dioscorides, Galen, Porphyry, Oppian, Polybius, Heliodorus, Aristotle,  
Aelian and others. When talking about such things, they would have  
the books of those authors brought to the table for greater certainty.  
And he so fully and totally kept in his memory what was said in them  
that at that time there would ==no physician who know half as much as==  
==he did.==

---
- **chivalry**: medieval code of conduct for knights. ethics/piety/manners
	- analogous to today's ettiquette
- many cases of literacy here
	- learning = building up literacy
	- the relevant passages and people mentioned are part of old french literature
		- only a few of them are part of the canon and facilitate discussion.
	- culinary literacy

# (ibid 282)
---
He hunted the stag, the roebuck, the bear, the deer, the wild-boar,  
the hare, the partridge, the pheasant, the bustard. He played with the  
weighted ball, sending it high in the air with fist or foot. He wrestled,  
ran and leapt, not doing any hop-skip-and-jumps, one-footed springs  
or German leaps (for, as Gymnaste said, such feats are of no use in  
war) but leaping over a ditch at a single bound, sailing over a hedge  
and springing six pace up the side of a wall, thus breaking through a  
window at the height of a lance.

---
- more literacy!
	- literacy in hunting
	- physical literacy
		- strength, gymnastics, etc.
		- **similar to chivalry** (getting ready for war)
- another snapshot of medieval culture

# (ibid 284-285)
---
Once grace was said, they would devote themselves to choral singing,  
to playing musical instruments or else to those lighter pastimes which  
use cards, dice or tumblers. And there they would remain, making  
good cheer and enjoying themselves, sometimes until bedtime; on  
other occasions they would drop in on gatherings of learned men or  
on folk who had visited foreign lands. Before retiring, once it was  
completely dark, they would go to the most open place in their  
lodgings to study the face of the sky, noting the comets,  
constellations and the locations, aspects, oppositions and  
conjunctions of the heavenly bodies. Then, in the manner of the  
Pythagoreans, he briefly recapitulated with his tutor every thing he  
had read, seen, learnt, done or heard throughout the livelong day.

---
- another snapshot of medieval times
- this time, less "important" literacies
	- literacy in playing, socializing even (called civic literacy) 
	- shows musical literacy
	- astronomy
	- mathematics
