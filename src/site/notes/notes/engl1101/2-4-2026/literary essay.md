## literacies.
- **media literacy** (p. 15)
	- **creates** pieces of media or **analyzes** it
	- able to find credibility, reliability, argument, etc.
- **digital literacy** (p. 17-18)
	- requires **electronic technology**
	- makes use of **internet**
	- relies on multiple codes
	- **requires research**
- **info literacy** (p. 19-20)
	- determines the extent of info needed
	- **access information** effectively + efficiently
	- evaluate info and sources **critically**
	- **use info** to accomplish specific purpose
- **multicultural literacy** (p. 24)
	- interaction with other cultures
	- learning about the **culture of others** and space you are in
	- **important terms** for multicultural literacy (p. 25):
		- **dominant culture** - mainstream value orientation of a society
		- **subculture** - part of any distinctive community
		- **group culture** - shared perspective and set of norms for a group
		- **counterculture** - "other" cultures within dominant culture.
	- "are you part of a fandom?"
- **community literacies** (p. 27)
	- similar to cultural literacies
	- **literacy practices** of specific groups
	- looks at shared and different **cultural aspects of life** that influence us
- **civil literacy** (p. 29)
	- the knowledge and ability capacity of citizens to make sense of their **political world**
	- law stuff
- **literacy in the academy** (p. 32)
	- the reason for english 1101
	- **academics**, literacy in learning
	- english for academic purposes (EAP) to join a **scholarly conversation** about a topic.

## what is considered a narrative?
- a **narrative** is the representation of an event or series of events. it can not be only a **description** of something, it must be an action; something that **took place**.
- **narrative** is the representation of events, consisting of **story** and **narrative** discourse.
	- **story**: event or sequence of events
	- **narrative discourse**: those events as represented.
		- - **narrative discourse** is the structured telling of events. it is a **meditated** presentation that uses techniques to influence **audience perception**.
			- "narrative discourse is infinitely malleable"
			- can be true or false, historical or fictional.
- "cinderella story"
	- archetype of story of someone/something that unexpectedly does well despite being mistreated.
	- (king lear)
