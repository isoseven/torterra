- look into a sacred/secular place
	- looking into *church / after hours youth room*
- what is unusual about it?
	- it's in the crossroads between *korean/american/immigrant culture*
- how is it **commodified**?
	- food distribution
	- operates as a gathering space
	- also acts as a holding period for adults to finish their service upstairs
	- creates social connections and bonding
- what do people tend to use it for?
	- eating
	- talking with/making new friends
	- playing games/entertainment
- what are the implications of being present at an event there?
	- being here = your parents are here
	- shared space (physically)
	- interaction with both korean (older generation immigrants) and english (those born here)
- are there ways in which the space can be modified?
	- yes, but it won't make much difference; the people will still act the same (better/worse conditions)
- what have scholars/researchers said about these kinds of areas?
	- ray oldenburg [third place](https://plannersweb.com/wp-content/uploads/1997/01/184.pdf)
